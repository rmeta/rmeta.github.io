Research Projects

  • Learning Generation Z and Millennial Preferences in Sustainable Fashion through Machine Learning
    This research focuses on understanding consumer preferences in sustainable fashion among Generation Z and Millennials. Using machine learning techniques, the project aims to derive insights into their attitudes and behavior toward sustainability. The results help in tailoring products and marketing strategies that align with these generations' values.
  • Applications of Operations Research in Wildfire Management
    This research explores how operations research can be used to optimize wildfire management strategies. It includes the application of network optimization and linear programming to allocate resources efficiently and minimize wildfire impact.
  • Exploring the Effects of Product Innovation on Circular Economy Initiatives
    The project investigates the balance between product innovation and product reusability in the context of Made to be Remade initiatives. It assesses how different strategies influence product lifecycle and contribute to sustainability goals.
  • Analysis of Consumer Behavior in Collaborative Clothing Consumption
    This project examines how Generation Z and Millennials perceive collaborative clothing consumption. It identifies the factors driving consumers towards sustainable choices and the influence of social and economic factors on their preferences.

Presentations

  • Learning Generation Z and Millennial Preferences in Sustainable Fashion - #CORS2024, London, ON
    Presented the findings of my research on consumer preferences in sustainable fashion, focusing on how machine learning can help understand the motivations of Generation Z and Millennials. The presentation covered data analysis methods and implications for sustainable fashion industries.
  • Operations Research for Wildfire Management - UBC, Kelowna, BC
    Delivered a detailed presentation on the applications of operations research techniques to wildfire management. It highlighted methods such as optimization modeling for effective resource allocation during wildfire incidents.
  • Sustainability in Fashion among Generation Z - Lululemon, Vancouver, BC
    Presented a study on sustainable fashion among Generation Z, discussing key motivations and barriers for young consumers in adopting sustainable fashion. The presentation aimed to help Lululemon align its products with the expectations of young, sustainability-conscious consumers.
